# AFTERBEFORE — ORGANIC SHARE & VIRAL GROWTH ENGINE

**Status:** Growth Model  
**Scope:** Shareable achievements, VS challenges, referrals, leaderboard sharing and Memento content  
**Primary Principle:** Users do not advertise AfterBefore. They share their own identity, achievements and memories, while AfterBefore grows through the story.

---

## 1. Core Thesis

AfterBefore should turn every meaningful user achievement into a shareable moment.

The user should never feel that they are posting an advertisement for the app.

They should feel that they are sharing:

- their achievement,
- their rank,
- their route,
- their rivalry,
- their contribution,
- their memory,
- their identity inside the scene.

> **The user is the main character. AfterBefore is the system that made the moment possible.**

---

## 2. Viral Growth Loop

```text
User completes a meaningful action
→ AfterBefore generates a shareable asset
→ user shares it on Instagram, TikTok or messaging apps
→ friends open the deep link
→ they view the achievement, route or challenge
→ they join the app or accept the challenge
→ the original user receives a referral signal or reward
→ new users create more activity
→ new activity creates more shareable moments
```

The engine should convert product usage into organic distribution.

## 5. Share as a Challenge

The most viral share is not:

> “Look what I did.”

It is:

> **“Can you do better?”**

Challenge formats:

- beat my weekly score,
- build a better roadmap,
- discover more verified venues,
- finish the same sponsored quest,
- complete more crew quests,
- overtake me before Monday,
- build a stronger Memento month,
- reach the next curator level first.

This turns each share into a product entry point.

## 6. Referral Attribution

Every share should contain a unique referral link.

The system should track:

- who shared,
- what was shared,
- where it was shared,
- who opened it,
- who signed up,
- who completed first check-in,
- who completed first quest,
- who returned the following week.

### Referral Funnel

```text
Share created
→ link opened
→ account created
→ first check-in
→ first quest
→ second-week return
→ confirmed referral
```

---

## 7. Referral Metrics

The primary metrics are not total share clicks.

The key metrics are:

- share-to-click rate,
- click-to-signup rate,
- signup-to-check-in rate,
- signup-to-first-quest rate,
- referral activation rate,
- referral retention,
- active users generated per share,
- cost equivalent of organic acquisition,
- top-performing share formats,
- top-performing creator segments.

---

## 8. Reward Logic

Users should not receive large rewards simply for pressing the Share button.

That would create spam.

### Recommended Reward Flow

#### Share Created
- no reward or symbolic XP.

#### Friend Opens Link
- tracked, but no major reward.

#### Friend Creates Account
- small referral XP.

#### Friend Completes First Check-In
- larger referral XP.

#### Friend Completes First Quest
- contributor or growth bonus.

#### Friend Returns Next Week
- confirmed referral reward.

### Core Rule

> **Reward completed growth, not empty sharing.**

## 12. Privacy Rules

Shareable content must never automatically expose:

- exact private locations,
- secret events,
- private afterparties,
- identities of other users without consent,
- detailed GPS trails,
- health information,
- safety reports,
- sensitive social interactions.

The user chooses whether a share contains:

- only result,
- public venues,
- approximate route,
- photos,
- opponent,
- crew name,
- city or district,
- exact date.

VS content requires consent from both sides before public sharing.

## 18. Anti-Spam Rules

The engine must prevent:

- repeated low-value sharing,
- self-referral,
- fake accounts,
- referral farms,
- manipulated clicks,
- purchased engagement,
- repeated identical shares,
- hidden sponsored content.

### Possible Controls

- no unlimited XP for share clicks,
- rewards only after meaningful referral actions,
- one referral reward per verified person,
- device and account pattern checks,
- delayed reward confirmation,
- trust requirement for larger rewards,
- audit log for major leaderboard rewards.
